GigaWorld-1: A Roadmap to
World Models for Robot Policy Evaluation

A systematic study of world models as policy evaluators, introducing WMBench and a practical design roadmap for building evaluator-oriented world models.

GigaAI  |  CVPR 2026

arXivPaper (PDF) Code 🤗DatasetBeta 🤗Model 📊Benchmark Coming Soon

Abstract

Evaluation remains a fundamental bottleneck for robot foundation model development. Unlike large language models, where models can be screened quickly through efficient online benchmarks, embodied policies often require repeated real-world rollouts that are slow, expensive, and limited by hardware availability and human supervision. This motivates the use of world models as policy evaluators: if a learned world model can faithfully preserve the relative success and failure of policy rollouts, it could substantially reduce the cost of policy iteration.


In this work, we present a systematic study of world models for robot policy evaluation and introduce WMBench, a benchmark built from real-robot teleoperation data and paired policy rollouts across eight manipulation task families. Using this benchmark, we study ten video world models, three action representation schemes, and more than 20,000 world-model policy rollouts together with paired real-world executions.


Our experiments reveal three main findings. First, evaluator quality depends much more on long-horizon, action-faithful rollout consistency than on short-horizon visual realism alone. Second, pretraining and data composition matter not only through scale, but through balancing broad world knowledge with robot-specific controllability. Third, architectural choices such as action representation, memory, and evaluator-oriented post-training substantially affect agreement with real-world outcomes. Based on these findings, we derive a practical design roadmap and instantiate it in GigaWorld-1.

Control Quality — Trajectory & Multi-View

Trajectory Accuracy. Trajectory accuracy under multi-view control. The same control signal is faithfully reflected in both head and wrist views, with sub-pixel alignment between predicted and ground-truth keypoints.

Precise Multi-View Control. Pixel-aligned, view-specific control signals keep every camera anchored to the same underlying action — head view follows the EE pose while the wrist view tracks its own ray map, so multi-view rollouts stay temporally and geometrically consistent over long horizons.

Transfer & Generalization

The same world-model design can be rapidly adapted to autonomous driving, supports edge / depth map conditioning, generalizes across different robot embodiments, and produces long-horizon rollouts of arbitrary length.

WMBench at a Glance

WMBench evaluates every rollout along 15 fine-grained metrics, organised across two axes: rollout source (rows) and evaluation dimensions (columns).

🌐

Multi-dimensional Evaluation

15 fine-grained metrics covering perceptual quality, temporal consistency, geometry, motion, and instruction grounding — a holistic view of every rollout.

🧠

Metrics + VLM

Pair deterministic metric scoring with vision-language model judgements to capture both measurable fidelity and semantic correctness of the rollout.

🔁

Closed-loop Policy Evaluation

Generated observations are fed back into the policy to produce the next action — paired against real-world rollouts for true evaluator agreement.
